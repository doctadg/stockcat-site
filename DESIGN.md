# STOCKCAT — Design Direction

## Thesis

A memecoin site disguised as the world’s most overstaffed stock-photo search engine. The joke lands in one line: **one cat, every job**. The cat is the product, the proof, and the interface.

## Character

Deadpan, overbooked, oddly professional. Internet-native rather than crypto-template degen. Every visual should look like it came from a contact sheet, commercial casting board, photocopied agency deck, or image-search result.

## Palette

- Paper `#F2EFE4` — warm stock-catalog substrate
- Ink `#111111` — keylines and primary type
- Cobalt `#183BFF` — system/action signal
- Highlighter `#E8FF32` — selection and mascot framing
- Record `#FF3B2F` — live/status dot only

## Type

- Display: Anton, narrow commercial-poster energy
- Editorial contrast: Instrument Serif Italic
- Interface: Geist Sans + Geist Mono
- Display tracking stays tight. UI labels are compact uppercase mono.

## Motion

Physical and finite: contact-sheet slides, image swaps, crop-mark pulses, ticker movement, slight card tilt. No neon glows, floating particles, or generic crypto orbs. Reduced-motion users get static final states.

## Interaction model

- Image-search hero with cycling query
- Clickable job catalog filters
- “Hire the cat” casting generator
- Contract status button that states the honest pre-launch state
- Pointer-responsive casting card with bounded tilt

## Anti-patterns

No fake prices, holders, market cap, contract, or live dashboard. No roadmap lore. No token coin render. No luxury editorial museum framing. No generic three-card “tokenomics” grid. No third-party stock-platform branding.
