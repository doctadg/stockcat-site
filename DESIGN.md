# STOCKCAT — Design Direction

## Thesis

A memecoin site built around the original stock-photo tabby. The proposition lands in one line: **the first Stockcat trades; holders share**. The cat is the product, the proof, and the interface.

## Character

Deadpan, overbooked, oddly professional. Internet-native rather than crypto-template degen. Every visual should look like it came from a contact sheet, commercial casting board, photocopied agency deck, or image-search result.

## Palette

- Paper `#F2EFE4` — warm stock-catalog substrate
- Ink `#111111` — keylines and primary type
- Cobalt `#183BFF` — system/action signal
- Highlighter `#E8FF32` — selection and mascot framing
- Record `#FF3B2F` — live/status dot only

## Type

- Display: Anton, narrow commercial-poster energy
- Editorial contrast: Instrument Serif Italic
- Interface: Geist Sans + Geist Mono
- Display tracking stays tight. UI labels are compact uppercase mono.

## Motion

The site is a single sideways contact sheet: six viewport-sized panels with native horizontal snap. Mouse wheel motion is translated to horizontal travel; touch uses direct swipe; keyboard arrows, Page Up/Down, Home, and End are supported. The 300-image archive is the only nested vertical scroll area. Panel changes use a finite darkroom-shutter wipe, blur-rise content staging, live progress, image-search swaps, scanning lines, crop marks, and contact-sheet parallax. No top bar, vertical page scroll, neon glows, floating particles, or generic crypto orbs. Reduced-motion users get static final states and immediate snapping.

## Interaction model

- Floating Stockcat mark and wordmark with no top bar
- Image-search hero with a cycling Stockcat preview
- 300-image Stockcat archive with lazy loading and source links
- Live Robinhood Stock Token market shelf
- Live Stock Token swap tape with explicit official/reference-wallet labeling
- Read-only wallet X-ray and deterministic buyback-vault attribution
- Honest pre-launch status throughout the trading and holder-share flow

## Anti-patterns

No fake prices, holders, market cap, contract, or live dashboard. No roadmap lore. No token coin render. No luxury editorial museum framing. No generic three-card “tokenomics” grid. No third-party stock-platform branding.
